EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

We are pleased to present an investment opportunity in a 399-unit multifamily property located in Boston, Massachusetts. The property is situated in a highly desirable location with strong demand for rental units, making it an attractive investment opportunity for both experienced and first-time investors.

The property is a Class A apartment complex that offers a mix of studio, one-, and two-bedroom units, with spacious floor plans and modern amenities. The property is well-maintained and in excellent condition, with recent upgrades to common areas and unit interiors. The property benefits from its prime location in a rapidly growing neighborhood with access to public transportation, major highways, and a variety of dining and entertainment options. The area is known for its strong employment and educational opportunities, making it a popular choice for young professionals and families.

The investment opportunity includes the acquisition of the property at an attractive price point with potential for value-add opportunities through operational efficiencies, targeted upgrades, and rental increases. The property has a strong historical occupancy and rent growth rate, and we anticipate continued strong demand for rental units in the area.

MARKET ANALYSIS

The property's location in a desirable neighborhood with strong demand for rental units is a key factor in its attractiveness as an investment opportunity. The neighborhood offers easy access to major employment centers, cultural attractions, and recreational amenities, making it a popular choice for young professionals and families. The area also has a limited supply of new multifamily construction, which is expected to support continued rent growth and high occupancy rates.

  • The city has a population of approximately 700,000 residents
  • Boston is home to numerous world-class universities, hospitals, and biotech firms, as well as a thriving financial services industry
  • Boston boasts consistently high occupancy rates and steady rent growth. According to a report by CBRE, the average occupancy rate for multifamily properties in Boston was 96.5% in 2023, up from 96.2% in 2022. The same report also indicates that rental rates in the city have increased by approximately 2.6% year-over-year.
  • According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the unemployment rate in the Boston-Cambridge-Newton metropolitan area was 2.7% as of March 2022 (the national average is 4.2%).
  • The population of the city is projected to grow by 4.6% between 2020 and 2025, according to a report by Marcus & Millichap.

RISK ANALYSIS

It's important to recognize that although we believe this deal to be an excellent one, it is not risk-free. Examples of risk factors include:

  • Market Risk: A decline in the Boston real estate market could negatively impact the property's value and rental income.
  • Renovation Risk: Unexpected costs or delays. We have incorporated reserves to mitigate this risk.
  • Tenant Risk: Occupancy depends on tenants' ability to pay rent and adhere to lease agreements. We conduct extensive background checks on all tenants.
  • Competition Risk: The property faces competition from other multifamily properties in the area, which could impact occupancy rates and rental income.
  • Interest Rate Risk: Changes in interest rates could impact the property's financing costs and overall profitability.
  • Regulatory Risk: Changes in local or state regulations could impact the property's operations or require additional compliance costs.
  • Natural Disaster Risk: The property may be at risk of damage from natural disasters such as hurricanes, floods, or earthquakes, which could result in unexpected repair costs and a disruption of operations.
